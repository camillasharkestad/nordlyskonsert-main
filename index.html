Current focus

ÁLFR — a Northern Lights Concert

Welcome to a magical concert performance at Forum Scene.

Majestic music, spectacular nature and dancing northern lights.

ÁLFR — a Northern Lights Concert is a scenic concert experience where Eilif Stene’s images of Norwegian nature and the aurora borealis form the visual thread throughout the performance.

The music and lyrics are composed by Jørn Lavoll and Marianne Juvik Sæbø, inspired by nature and the northern lights.

The atmospheric music moves in the borderland between game music and film music. On this occasion, it will be performed by the Royal Norwegian Navy Band, pianist and choir, conducted by Bjørn Sagstad. The music has been rearranged for concert band by Reid Gilje.

The vocalist is Marianne Juvik Sæbø.

About Marianne

Marianne Juvik Sæbø

Marianne Juvik Sæbø is an experienced and versatile singer based in Bergen/Askøy. For many years, she has touched audiences with her warm voice, musical confidence, and strong ability to communicate through song. With her education from Bergen Music Conservatory/The Grieg Academy and the Royal Academy of Music in London, Marianne has a solid classical background, while her repertoire spans widely — from hymns, traditional songs, folk music, and gospel to classical works, musical theatre, and opera.

Marianne accepts singing engagements for a wide range of occasions, both large and small. She performs at concerts, memorial gatherings, church ceremonies, and private events, placing great emphasis on creating a musical setting that suits the occasion.

At funerals and memorial services, Marianne contributes with singing that allows room for comfort, dignity, and reflection. She can perform well-known hymns, cherished songs, or music that held special meaning for the deceased and their loved ones. With respect for the moment and care for the family, she wishes to make the music a beautiful and meaningful part of the farewell.

To book Marianne for a singing engagement, concert, or ceremony, you are welcome to get in touch for a conversation about repertoire, occasion, and practical details.
